Overview

During the summer and fall of 2020, I had the opportunity to join Sovos as an UX Design Intern. Over the past months, I worked with a team of 7 UX designers. I collaborated with cross-functional teams to ideate and brainstorm, to conduct usability testing, and to develop prototypes to hand off to development.

Project 2 - TIR Enterprise

The purpose of the TIR Enterprise software is to help ensure clients are compliant with 1099 reporting. It automates error-prone manual processes, and alerts clients of regulatory changes. Sovos is the largest filer of 1099 forms in the world!

The product that I worked on is meant to support clients in verifying TIN records against the IRS database, and soliciting missing TIN information. I participated in stakeholder meetings, and collaborated with lead designers to develop the final interface.

Project workflow. Images are blurred for confidentiality.

I facilitated usability testing sessions, and was able to learn a lot by speaking with users with expertise in the compliance process. I was able to diagnose issues in the prototype, which I recorded in an excel sheet.

Images are blurred for confidentiality.

TAKEAWAYS

Because I onboarded this project after much of the initial research and brainstorming was done, the learning curve here was huge for me. Through this experience, I recognized the importance of proactively reaching out to cross-functional leadership regularly, and consistently facilitating collaboration between everyone.

Project 3 - Ship Compliant‚Äôs New Dashboard

The Ship Compliant line of business helps distributors be compliant of taxes during the shipment of alcohol and beverages, as well as a variety of other goods. It automates sales tax against regulations to make sure that all shipments are legal. One of Sovos‚Äô biggest clients in this space is FedEx!

Ship Compliant has an old dashboard (over 9 years old!). I was responsible for the design of the new dash. I collaborated with lead designers, product managers, and development to create the final interface.

Receiving feedback on my work through Miro. Images are blurred for confidentiality.

TAKEAWAYS

Ship Compliant is a legacy product with no design system, and I was allowed the freedom to design all components and interactions on the page. It was super exciting to design with little constraints!
